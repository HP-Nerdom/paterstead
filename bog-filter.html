Background

How a Bog Filter Works

A bog filter (also called a wetland filter) is a biological filtration system where water is pumped up through a shallow gravel or lava rock bed planted with aquatic or bog plants. As water moves slowly through the media, beneficial bacteria colonize the substrate and break down ammonia and nitrites. The plants uptake nutrients directly, completing the nitrogen cycle naturally.

Key Benefits

  • › Natural biological filtration — low maintenance once established
  • › Plants double as food sources (watercress, cattails, etc.)
  • › No chemical inputs required
  • › Works well as part of an aquaponics or pond system
  • › Can be scaled from a small container pond to a large backyard pond

General Sizing Rule of Thumb

The bog filter should be roughly 10–20% of the surface area of the pond or fish tank it's filtering. Pump turnover rate is typically the full pond volume once every 1–2 hours.
