Mealworm Farm

A low-effort, continuous protein source for chickens and other animals. Minimal space, minimal smell when well-maintained.

Overview

How It Works

Mealworms are the larval stage of the darkling beetle (Tenebrio molitor). You maintain a rotating colony of 4 bins at different life stages. When a bin completes its cycle, you compost it and start a new one, keeping the colony going continuously.

Life Cycle Reminder Egg → Larva (mealworm) → Pupa → Adult beetle → Eggs again. The whole cycle takes a few months at room temperature. Warmer = faster.

Setup

Supplies

What You Need

  • Smooth plastic tub (~6" deep) — mealworms cannot climb smooth plastic
  • Wheat bran — 2–3" layer acts as both bedding and food
  • 500+ starter mealworms or beetles (avoid "giant mealworms" — treated with growth hormones)
  • Vegetable scraps: carrot or potato peels for moisture
  • Lid or mesh cover

Getting Started

Setting Up Your Farm

  1. Prepare the bin. Clean and dry your plastic tub. Add a 2–3" layer of wheat bran.
  2. Add worms or beetles. Place starter mealworms or beetles on top of the bran. Cover with a lid or mesh to keep things tidy.
  3. Add moisture. Place a chunk of carrot or potato peel on top for moisture — about one large potato piece per big bin or a 4" piece of carrot for a small tub. No free-standing water.

Ongoing Care

Feeding, Hydration & Rotation

Feeding and Hydration

Replace the vegetable scraps every 4–8 days. Remove old pieces before adding new ones to prevent mold. The wheat bran is the primary food — top it off as it gets consumed. No standing water ever in the bin.

The 4-Bin Rotation

Keep up to 4 bins going at a time at staggered stages. Every 4–6 weeks, transfer adult beetles and pupae to a fresh bin (they'll lay eggs there). Leave some behind in the original bin for continuity. When you're ready to start a 5th bin, dump the oldest bin (Bin 1) into your compost or garden — it's excellent fertilizer.

Why Separate Beetles From Worms Adult beetles will eat eggs and small worms if kept together too long. Moving them to a fresh bin at the right time keeps each stage healthy and the colony productive.

Environment & Harvest

Conditions & Harvesting

Ideal Conditions

FactorTarget
Temperature65–70°F (18–21°C)
LightDark or low light
SmellNone when well-maintained
HumidityModerate — provided by veggie scraps

Harvesting

Scoop mealworms directly from the bin for immediate feeding. For storing, you can refrigerate mealworms to slow their development. Move pupae or beetles to a separate bin to maintain the breeding colony while using the mealworms themselves for feed.

Uses

Feed fresh to chickens as a high-protein treat. Also suitable for reptiles, fish, and wild birds. The frass (waste material / depleted bran) is excellent garden fertilizer.

Quick Troubleshooting Mold in the bin usually means too much moisture from the veggie scraps — cut the pieces smaller or switch to drier options. No activity usually means it's too cold — move the bin somewhere warmer.
